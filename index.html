C.S. Lewis

1898 - 1963


A picture of C.S. Lewis

Worlds of Thought

C.S. Lewis: a writer of novels, stories, theology, apologetics and the like. He was a close friend and contemporary to the famous J.R.R. Tolkien who penned the Lord of the Rings series.

Perhaps C.S. Lewis is most famously known for writing the Chronicles of Narnia series but the list of his writings extends far beyond. He truly had worlds of thought and poured it out with pen and paper.

You are never too old to set another goal or to dream a new dream.
- C.S. Lewis

A Brief Biography

Clive Staples Lewis was born in Ireland in Belfast to Albert and Florence Lewis. At the age of 9 his mother died from cancer. After graduating he went to English for University but was sent off to fight in the war which encouraged him more towards atheism. After returning from the war and recovering from some injuries, he returned to his studies.

Lewis was greatly cared for by a fellow cadet's mother, Jane Moore. Lewis had made a pact to care for the family if his fellow cadet died in the war, and so it was. He grew incredibly fond of Jane, even introducing her as his mother. This was a very important relationship for Lewis.

After being persuaded by friends and philosophical arguments, Lewis returned to Christianity. Later, he married Joy Davidman Gresham but she died some years later from cancer. Lewis himself died just before his 65th birthday after several health difficulties.

True humility is not thinking less of yourself; it is thinking of yourself less.
- C.S. Lewis
